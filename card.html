Jellyfish

Jellyfish contain a hydrostatic skeleton that enables them to contract the muscles within their body or "bell", shooting water in the opposite direction for mobility.

  • Scientific Name: Cnidarians
  • Diet: Fish eggs, fish larvae, and plankton
  • Bell Size: From two-tenths of an inch to 6.5'
  • Lifespan: Between a few hours to two years

Jellyfish are cnidarians, named after the Greek word for "sea nettle". They are identified by their jelly-like bodies and tentacles that contain cnidocytes, which are cells on their tentacles that literally explode when they come into contact with prey.
